Kikuo Ibe was CASIO’s leading watch designer in the 1980s.


After inadvertently shattering a watch given to him by his father, Kikuo Ibe decided to create an indestructible watch.

In 1981, lbe and two others formed project team “Tough.” Their task was to create a watch that met the “Triple 10” criteria: the watch had to survive a 10-meter drop, withstand 10 atmospheres of water pressure, and have a battery life of 10 years.

The team had trouble realizing this vision, until one day Ibe observed a group of children bouncing a rubber ball in a park. This inspired the team to develop a watch in which the internal module is suspended in a hollow case, buffered at key points by a shock-absorbing rubber material. The watch itself is then given a rubber outer casing, further protecting it from impact.

After nearly 200 prototypes, the G-Shock DW-5000C went on sale in 1983.
